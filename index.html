Travel

Carry a Roam — a battery-powered touchscreen with a Summon button on the side — and the system at home travels with you. Same AI. Same photos. Same person, one tap away. Plug in a Latch Mobile and you can fix a friend's tower or a hotel printer with the same little kit.

Needs: a Roam in your bag, plus a Latch Mobile when you need to reach something that isn't yours.

FAQ

Reasonable questions.

How is the software free?

Because it runs on your hardware, not ours. We sell hardware (the CEC naval lineup) and we sell minutes of support from a real technician. The software, your local AI, and the always-on 1 Mbps relay cost us almost nothing per user, so we don't bill for them. If you ever want faster relay throughput or guaranteed time with a technician, those are the things you pay for — and only those.

How private is "private"?

Your local AI runs on your own brain — laptop, Counter, Probe, whatever. We can't read what it sees. When you press Summon, our AI gets only the one-sentence summary needed to route the ticket. The human on shift gets only the specific screen or device you approve, only while the session is live. We can see that a session happened (for billing). We don't record what was on it.

Will my existing stuff work — old printer, off-brand cameras, weird smart-home gear?

Probably. Anything with a standards-based interface (a network printer, an RTSP camera, Matter, Zigbee, HomeKit, generic USB) joins through your brain directly. Anything stranger — a printer with a USB cable, a tower without our app, a peripheral your laptop pretends doesn't exist — joins through a Latch Mobile. The point is to bring whatever you've already paid for into the fold, not to make you replace it.

What if I'm not technical at all?

That's the entire point. The brain handles the networking. The AI handles the watching. Summon hands you a real person when something's beyond either. You shouldn't have to learn what an IP address is to make a printer print — and with this, you don't.

What happens if my internet goes out?

Your brain and its reach are on your local network, so they keep talking to each other. Cameras still show. Printers still print. Your local AI keeps watching. You lose remote access from outside the house (reaching the home server from the airport) and Summon (which needs to reach our humans). Both come back the moment the internet does.

Am I locked in if I buy the hardware?

No. The software works on anything you already own, so you don't have to buy anything to start. If you do buy hardware from us — a Bell, a Roam, the Probe, a CEC heavyweight — it's yours forever. Cancel anytime and every box keeps running the app and your local AI. Your data has always lived on your devices, not ours, so there's nothing on our side for you to lose.

Why is Latch Mobile $99 if Latch Pro is also $99?

Different shapes of the same idea. Mobile is external — sits beside any machine over USB and HDMI, portable, not tied to a host. Pro is internal — PCIe, fixed to one machine, deeper access (BIOS, fans, power). Different tradeoffs, comparable bill of materials. Every CEC machine ships with a Latch Pro already inside; you'd buy a standalone Pro to put one in a non-CEC tower.

Is this just Tailscale or a VPN?

Opposite premise. Tailscale gives you a virtual network to administer — useful if you already know what to do with one. We give you the outcomes: print from anywhere, see your cameras, restore a new laptop, render-over-there, fix-it-from-the-road. There's no tunnel to configure and no IPs to remember; the brain handles all of that.
