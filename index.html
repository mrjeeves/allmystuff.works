FAQ

Reasonable questions.

Is the Den+ really a cloud-gaming rig?

Yes — that's a first-class scenario. The GPU lives in Den+; your laptop or tablet is a thin client. Steam library, AAA titles, low-latency streaming inside your home over your LAN. On the road, the same trick over our relay (Stream or Cascade tier helps).

Why are Latch and Latch Pro the same price?

They're different shapes of the same idea. Latch is external — USB-and-HDMI beside a machine, portable, not tied to a host. Latch Pro is internal — PCIe, deeper access, fixed to one machine. Different tradeoffs, comparable bill of materials. Den and Den+ ship with a Latch Pro already inside.

What does the local AI actually do?

Watches your stuff so a person doesn't have to — sorting photos, noticing the printer's offline, hearing the cameras drop out, summarizing what a Summon ticket needs to say. Runs on Counter, Den, or Den+. We can't see it. We don't keep it.

Do I have to buy any of this hardware?

No. The software runs on what you already own. The boxes exist for jobs the things in your life don't already do well — clip-on recording, render-over-there, fix-when-the-OS-won't-boot. Skip all of them if you like.

Why no "named concierge" tier?

Because that's what makes everyone else's plan cost $200/month. Your AI is the continuity. Whoever's on shift can pick up a ticket and be up to speed in seconds. If you genuinely need a dedicated human, talk to us — but the price will reflect it.
