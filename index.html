FAQ

Reasonable questions.

What does the local AI actually do?

It watches your devices for you — sorting photos, noticing the printer's offline, hearing the camera dropouts — so a human (us, or you) doesn't have to. It runs on your own hardware. We can't see it; we don't keep it.

So how does a human help, if they can't see anything?

When you press Summon, your AI prepares a minimum-viable picture of the problem: the error, the relevant log line, the one screen that matters. A human gets that — not your house. If we need more, the device asks you, in plain English, before showing it.

Do I have to buy your hardware?

No. The software runs on what you own. Hardware exists for capture, record, route, process, and act — five jobs the things in your life don't already do well. Skip all of it if you like.

Why is the free tier actually free?

Because the expensive part of helping people is human time, and the local AI cuts a lot of it out. Software, relay (slowly), and your AI cost us little. We charge for speed when bits move through us, and minutes when a person is in the loop. That's it.

What happens if I cancel?

Your devices keep talking to each other when they can reach each other directly. Your AI keeps running on your hardware. You drop to the free relay and pay-per-session Summon. Nothing on your devices vanishes — we never had it.
