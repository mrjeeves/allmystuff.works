FAQ

Reasonable questions.

Why at least one brain — can't every device be its own?

The brain hosts your local AI, which needs always-on horsepower and a steady network address. Phones sleep, tablets sleep, Bells are terminals. Pick at least one box that does the thinking, and the system has somewhere to live. That can be a laptop you leave docked, a small Counter ($499), or any naval-class rig.

Can I have more than one brain?

You should. Each brain you add is another stream of work the system can run in parallel — game streaming on one while your AI sorts photos on another, run home automations on a third, monitor the workshop with a fourth. Travel with one (a Roam), leave another at home (a Counter or Probe). They coordinate automatically; the AI follows you across them. None of this is subscription-gated. You pay for the hardware, you own the capacity. Stack as you can afford to.

What's the difference between a limb and the brain?

The brain runs the software AND hosts the AI. Limbs do one specific job each — summon (Bell), capture voice (Lapel), sense (Pulse), bridge other machines in (Latch Mobile / Pro). A second brain-class device (a second Counter, say) just becomes another limb that happens to be capable of brain duty.

What's "Den+ rated" mean exactly?

A CEC naval-class machine earns "Den+ rated" when it ships with a workstation-class or Nvidia GPU — that's Destroyer ($3,499) and up. The GPU lets your laptop become a thin client for AAA gaming, AI inference, or render workloads. The Probe is "Den" (no plus) — great as a brain, not as a remote-render rig.

What happens if I cancel?

Your brain keeps running. Your AI keeps watching. Your limbs keep talking to the brain. You drop to the free relay and the pay-per-session Summon rate. Nothing on your devices vanishes — we never had it.
