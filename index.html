Play

Latch Pro lives inside Den+. It watches the boot screen, the fans, the BIOS — your AI files a ticket before you'd notice. When you press Summon, a human shows up already up to speed. Carry Latch to a friend's machine for the same trick, no install.

Roam is a battery Pi 5 with ports. Plug in the camera, the mic, the weird USB device — and reach it from a different room or a different city. The little box does the routing; your AI does the rest.

Uses: Roam · any device you'd rather not carry.

FAQ

Reasonable questions.

Is the Den+ really a cloud-gaming rig?

Yes — that's a first-class scenario. The GPU lives in Den+; your laptop or tablet acts as a thin client. Steam library, AAA titles, low-latency streaming inside your home over your LAN. On the road, the same trick over our relay (Stream or Cascade tier helps).

Why is Latch the same price as Latch Pro?

They're different shapes of the same idea. Latch is external — USB-and-HDMI beside a machine, portable, not tied to a host. Latch Pro is internal — PCIe, deeper access, fixed to one machine. Different tradeoffs, comparable bill of materials. Den and Den+ ship with a Latch Pro inside.

What does the local AI actually do?

Watches your stuff so a person doesn't have to — sorting photos, noticing the printer's offline, hearing the cameras drop out, summarizing what a Summon ticket needs to say. Runs on Counter, Den, or Den+. We can't see it. We don't keep it.

Do I have to buy any of this hardware?

No. The software runs on what you already own. The hardware exists for jobs the things in your life don't already do well — clip-on recording, render-over-there, fix-when-the-OS-won't-boot. Skip all of it if you like.

Why no "named concierge" tier?

Because that's what makes everyone else's plan cost $200/month. Your AI is the continuity. Whoever's on shift can pick up a ticket and be up to speed in seconds. If you genuinely need a dedicated human, talk to us — but the price will reflect it.
