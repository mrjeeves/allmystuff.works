Networks

Your own little cloud. Without the cloud.

A network is your private mesh — the devices and services you choose, talking directly to each other. No central server pretending to be the source of truth. You hold the keys; we just help the packets find each other.

Peer-to-peer, by design

Your devices connect directly when they can — through Trystero on top of WebRTC. We never sit in the data path between them.

STUN & TURN, included

stun.allmystuff.works and turn.allmystuff.works are part of every plan, including the free tier. Authenticated per account so freeloaders don't bog down your throughput.

Bring any device

Phones, laptops, that Raspberry Pi in the closet, a VPS, a tablet your kid uses. The moment you install our app, it joins your network. No accounts to wire up, no ports to forward.

Quiet by default

We can see that your devices are talking. We can't see what they say. Your network's traffic is end-to-end encrypted between peers.

FAQ

Reasonable questions, honest answers.

Why do I need an account just to use the free tier?

Because stun.allmystuff.works and turn.allmystuff.works are real servers we run, and unauthenticated relays are catnip for abuse. An account lets us throttle per-account rather than block everyone on a noisy ISP. Free still means free — we just want to know which "you" is using it.

What exactly do you throttle on the free tier?

Per-second throughput, not total bandwidth. You can move as many bytes as you like over a month; you just can't move them all in one explosive burst. In practice this is plenty for chat, sync, voice, and most file-shuffling. It starts to pinch on heavy video relay through TURN — that's where the paid plans help.

Is this a Tailscale / ZeroTier?

Those give you a virtual network — that's one slice of what we do. We bundle that with the app that makes your devices useful on it, hardware to buy if you'd rather not jury-rig, and a real human you can summon when something stops behaving. Different scope, not just a thinner pipe.

Do I have to buy your hardware?

No. The app runs on what you already own — laptop, phone, NAS, a Pi, a beat-up tower. Hardware is for filling gaps: coverage in a stubborn room, a quiet always-on server, a workshop machine. Plenty of customers never buy any.

Can you see what's on my screen during a Summon session?

Only the human helper assigned to your session, only the device you picked, only while the session is active, and only with a fresh confirmation tap on the device being viewed. We can see that a session happened (for billing); we don't record its contents.

Can I run my own TURN server?

Yes, on Workshop and above. You point your clients at your own URL and we stop billing you for relay. People mostly don't bother — running a TURN that's actually reliable is more work than it sounds.

What happens if I cancel?

Your network keeps working, peer to peer. You lose access to our STUN/TURN beyond the free tier and to Summon. Hardware you bought is yours forever — it just falls back to free-tier throughput. Nothing on your devices vanishes; we never had it.
