How it works

Three steps. No new things to administer.

  1. 1

    Install the app where you are

    Phone, laptop, tablet — whatever you tend to be at. This is the screen the rest of your stuff is going to reach you on.

  2. 2

    Install it on what you want to reach

    The home server. The printer's controlling Pi. The work laptop. The always-on box in the closet. The app announces what each one can do — print, store, show, record — and your other devices can use it.

  3. 3

    Use it. Summon when you'd rather not.

    No port forwarding. No VPN. No "did you check the IP?" If something won't work — or you'd just rather a human did it — press Summon. We're a concierge, not a chatbot.

FAQ

Reasonable questions, honest answers.

Do I need to understand networking?

No. That's the entire point. Install our app on the devices you care about, use them. If something doesn't work, press Summon and a real human handles it. You should never have to learn what a port is.

Is this a Tailscale / ZeroTier?

No, and we're not trying to be. They give you a virtual network — useful if you know what to do with one, and a lot of exposure if you don't. We give you outcomes: print from anywhere, see your cameras, reach the other machine, restore from a backup. No VPN for you to administer.

So how do you reach my other devices, then?

Each device runs our app, which announces what it can do (print, store, show, record) to the other devices on your account. When you ask one device for something the other can do, they talk directly, peer-to-peer, only for as long as needed. No new always-on network for you to forget about and worry about.

Why do I need an account just to use the free tier?

When your devices can't reach each other directly, we relay the traffic — and unauthenticated relays are catnip for abuse. An account lets us throttle per-account rather than block everyone on a noisy ISP. Free still means free; we just need to know which "you" is using it.

What gets throttled on the free tier?

How fast the traffic moves through us, per second. Not how much. You can do as much over a month as you like — you just can't blast it through at full speed. Plenty for printing, sync, voice, and most file-shuffling. Heavy video starts to want a paid tier.

Do I have to buy your hardware?

No. The app runs on what you already own — laptop, phone, NAS, a Pi, a beat-up tower. Hardware is for filling gaps: a printer with no brains, a camera with no app worth using, a stubborn corner of the house. Plenty of customers never buy any.

Can you see what's on my screen during a Summon session?

Only the human helper assigned to your session, only the device you picked, only while the session is active, and only after a fresh confirmation tap on the device being viewed. We can see that a session happened (for billing); we don't record its contents.

What happens if I cancel?

Your devices still talk to each other when they can reach each other directly. You lose our relay beyond the free tier, and you lose Summon. Hardware you bought is yours forever — it just falls back to free-tier relay speeds. Nothing on your devices vanishes; we never had it.
