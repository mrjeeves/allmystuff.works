Networks

Your own little cloud. Without the cloud.

A network is your private mesh — the devices and services you choose, talking directly to each other. No central server pretending to be the source of truth. You hold the keys; we just help the packets find each other.

Peer-to-peer, by design

Your devices connect directly when they can — through Trystero on top of WebRTC. We never sit in the data path between them.

STUN & TURN, included

stun.allmystuff.works and turn.allmystuff.works are part of every plan, including the free tier. Authenticated per account so freeloaders don't bog down your throughput.

Bring any device

Phones, laptops, that Raspberry Pi in the closet, a VPS, a tablet your kid uses. If it speaks WebRTC — and most modern things do — it can join.

Quiet by default

We can see that your devices are talking. We can't see what they say. Your network's traffic is end-to-end encrypted between peers.

How it works

Three small steps, one calm cloud.

  1. 1

    Make an account

    Email, a passphrase, done. Your free account unlocks our STUN and TURN — throttled per second, not capped by month, so it always works, even at the end of the month.

  2. 2

    Spin up a network

    Name it. Invite your other devices with a QR code or a one-time link. They join the mesh and start gossiping about which agents they run and what they can do.

  3. 3

    Put your stuff to work

    Files, models, notes, scripts, a personal LLM, a backup target — anything one of your devices runs is available to the others. Need a hand? Summon a real human.

Cloud-mesh agents

A friendly fork of the agents you might already know.

We maintain a fork of myownllm.net's cloud-mesh agent framework — same shape, same APIs, with a few additions we needed in order to summon a human into your living room without it being weird.

What you get from upstream

  • Drop-in agents that announce themselves on the mesh
  • Capability discovery between peers
  • Streaming results across devices
  • Local-first model and tool orchestration

What we added

  • Consent-gated remote view of a device's screen
  • Consent-gated remote control with one-tap revoke
  • A handoff channel for a real human helper
  • Audit log on the device — not on our servers

Every remote-view session asks again, in person, on the device being viewed. There's no silent helper mode. There never will be.

Summon a human

The bit that makes us a concierge.

Some problems aren't tickets. They're "why is the printer doing that" and "I think I broke email" and "I'd just like someone to look at this with me for ten minutes." Press the button, and a real, vetted human shows up on your network — only the device you pick, only for as long as you say.

First

You press Summon

Pick what's bothering you from a short list, or type it out. Pick which device you want them to see. Nothing happens until you say so.

Then

They get a temporary, scoped peer on your network — not a screen-share through a third party. The session ends when you close it. Or when the timer runs out. Whichever comes first.

After

You decide what stays

Audit trail is on your device. The helper's notes, if you want them, are saved to your network. We keep a receipt for billing, and that's it.

FAQ

Reasonable questions, honest answers.

Why do I need an account just to use the free tier?

Because stun.allmystuff.works and turn.allmystuff.works are real servers we run, and unauthenticated relays are catnip for abuse. An account lets us throttle per-account rather than block everyone on a noisy ISP. Free still means free — we just want to know which "you" is using it.

What exactly do you throttle on the free tier?

Per-second throughput, not total bandwidth. You can move as many bytes as you like over a month; you just can't move them all in one explosive burst. In practice this is plenty for chat, sync, voice, and most file-shuffling. It starts to pinch on heavy video relay through TURN — that's where the paid plans help.

Is this a Tailscale / Zerotier?

Not quite. Those give you a virtual network at the IP layer. We give you a peer mesh at the application layer over WebRTC, with cloud-mesh agents on top. Closer cousins: Trystero, libp2p, IPFS. The headline difference is the human-on-tap part.

You're forking myownllm.net's cloud-mesh agents — why?

Their framework is great, and most of our work is upstream-compatible. We add a handful of things specific to remote viewing and to summoning a human, plus tighter consent prompts. We send fixes back when we can.

Can you see what's on my screen during a Summon session?

Only the helper assigned to your session, only the device you picked, only while the session is active, and only with a fresh confirmation tap on the device being viewed. We can see that a session happened (for billing); we don't record its contents.

Can I run my own TURN server?

Yes, on Workshop and above. You point your clients at your own URL and we stop billing you for relay. People mostly don't bother — running a TURN that's actually reliable is more work than it sounds.

What happens if I cancel?

Your network keeps working, peer to peer. You lose access to our STUN/TURN beyond the free tier and to Summon. Nothing on your devices vanishes — we never had it.
